What if language homogenizes due to the increased use of generative language?

All these threats go back to targeting the simplicity of language itself. While language can attributed as the principle form of communication, the emergence of technology may come to over-simplify this structure. In curating this claim, I used ideologies introduced by Terry Winograd in his regards to artificial intelligence. Winograd uses this work to discuss the future role of artificial intelligence, with researchers predicting that “thinking machines” will come to take over our “mental work”. While it is argued that computers cannot be creative and insightful, Winograd argues for the rethinking of these concepts in suggesting future designs to integrate human concerns and values. If the idea of these “cognitive artifacts” becomes successful in taking into account human activities and social practice, what if this use of technology simplifies the “mental work” of language in general? The use of these technologies can then in turn lead to a more uniform or standardized form of communication, raising the question, "What if language homogenizes due to the increased use of generative language?"

Language as a Privacy

In considering this future, there are a few notable outcomes:

  • Loss of Linguistic Diversity.

    • If generative language technologies become dominant, there might be a tendency for languages to converge into a more standardized form. This could result in a reduction of linguistic diversity as unique languages and dialects may be overshadowed.

  • Impact on Cultural Identity.

    • Through this, comes impacts on cultural identities. As language is often closely tied to cultural identity, if generative language technologies contribute to language homogenization, it may have an impact on cultural diversity. The effected cultures, in this case, will likely be minority communities that are unable to voice their concerns. Communities may need to navigate changes in how their languages are used and preserved.

  • Ethical and Bias Considerations.

    • In referring back to Winograd, generative language models are trained on large datasets that do not currently take into account human values. If not carefully curated, they may perpetuate biases present in the data. The homogenization of language could inadvertently propagate and reinforce certain biases, impacting the way people express themselves within society.

  • Evolution of New Language Norms.

    • The increased use of generative language could lead to the evolution of new language norms and conventions. People might adapt and innovate within the constraints of these technologies, creating unique linguistic expressions. These concepts lead to many more questions within themselves such as, "What if a new language is made?", or "What if all language reverts to form of emoticon/emoji?"
